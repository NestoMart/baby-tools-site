FAQ

Common Questions

Everything parents ask about calculating and tracking baby age.

To calculate your baby's age in weeks, subtract the birth date from today's date to get the total number of days, then divide by 7. Our calculator does this automatically and shows the exact weeks and remaining days — updated live every second.
Baby's age in months is crucial for tracking developmental milestones, scheduling vaccinations, and following feeding guidelines. Pediatricians use age in months up to 24 months to monitor healthy growth and development, making accurate tracking essential for every parent.
Chronological age is counted from the actual birth date. Corrected age (adjusted age) is used for premature babies and is calculated from the original due date. Corrected age is important for tracking milestones in preemie babies until about age 2, as it accounts for the time spent developing outside the womb earlier than expected.
Our baby age calculator is extremely accurate. It calculates the precise number of days between the birth date and today, then converts to weeks and months. The live counter updates every second to give real-time age tracking. No rounding — you get the exact age with remaining days clearly displayed.
Currently, our calculator shows the current age from the birth date up to today. Future dates cannot be selected as the birth date to ensure accuracy. For calculating a due date or planning future milestones, use our Due Date Calculator and Feeding Schedule tools in the Related Tools section above.
